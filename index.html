Featured Project

SEAL Team Project: Adaptive Management Plan for Seals near Churchill, Manitoba

Project Overview

This project was completed as part of the Students Engaged in Applied Learning (SEAL) Team at the University of Manitoba. The team members were Paige A. Newcomb, Emma R. Burton, Conner R. O’Dowda, and Dev K. Dave.

Together, we developed an adaptive management plan focused on bearded, ringed, and harbour seals near Churchill, Manitoba. The plan addressed climate change, vessel traffic, tourism pressure, long-term monitoring, and conservation-focused management responses.

This was a collaborative project, and I want to give equal credit to the full team for the research, planning, writing, and development of the final management plan.

Team Approach

Our work combined wildlife science, policy thinking, environmental monitoring, stakeholder awareness, and practical conservation planning. We worked together to build a project that connected research with real management recommendations.

I contributed as one member of a four-person SEAL team, and the final project reflects a shared effort across research, discussion, writing, and planning.

Why It Matters to Me

This experience reflects the kind of environmental work I value most: collaborative, applied, and grounded in real ecological challenges. It strengthened my interest in wildlife conservation, impact assessment, and team-based problem solving.
